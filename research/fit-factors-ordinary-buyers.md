## 1. Top Decision Factors for AI Agents (The Reality)
When mapping the agent landscape, these are the core factors that dictate whether a user abandons an agent or integrates it into their daily life:

*   **Ease of Setup (Friction):** Can they talk to it in 30 seconds, or do they need API keys, Discord, or a specific app download?
*   **Access Surface (Mobile vs. Web):** Does it live natively on their phone (WhatsApp/Telegram/iMessage) or do they have to open a browser tab?
*   **Tone & Personality (Vibe):** Does it sound like a corporate HR rep, a subservient assistant, a peer, or a coach?
*   **Memory Continuity:** Does it remember them tomorrow? Does it build context over time, or does it reset every session?
*   **Price Model:** Is it free with limits, subscription-based ($20/mo), or pay-per-use?
*   **Voice/Multimodal Capability:** Can they speak to it while driving or cooking? 
*   **Privacy & Data Use:** Is their data used to train models, or is it isolated?
*   **Capabilities (Actions vs. Chat):** Can it actually *do* things (book a calendar, send an email) or just *talk* about things?

## 2. Factors That Matter Most to Beginners
For ordinary buyers, the technical backend is irrelevant. The decision hierarchy is entirely emotional and practical:

1.  **Frictionless Access (Zero Setup):** If they have to watch a YouTube tutorial to log in, they will churn. They want it where they already are (iMessage, WhatsApp, Telegram, native app).
2.  **Personality & Tone:** Beginners feel awkward talking to a machine. If the agent sounds robotic or pedantic, they stop using it. They want a vibe match.
3.  **Voice Interaction:** Texting is work; talking is natural. Voice capability is a massive driver for non-technical adoption.
4.  **Memory:** They want the illusion of relationship. "It remembers what I told it yesterday" is the single biggest "magic trick" for a beginner.

## 3. Factors That Are Overhyped (Confusion Traps)
These metrics dominate tech Twitter but paralyze normal buyers:

*   **Model Benchmarks (GPT-4 vs. Claude 3.5 vs. Gemini):** Ordinary users cannot tell the difference between top-tier models for daily tasks. Pushing them to choose an LLM causes decision paralysis.
*   **Context Window Size (128k vs 1M):** Irrelevant unless they are uploading textbooks or coding repositories. 
*   **"Reasoning" vs. "Creativity" Toggles:** Beginners don't want to tweak parameters. They just want the right answer.
*   **Open Source vs. Closed Source:** The ideological debate does not matter to someone trying to plan a grocery run or draft a difficult email.

## 4. Proposed Shortlist: The First 5 Guiding Questions for AskStella
To guide an ordinary buyer to the right agent, AskStella's logic tree should strip away the tech jargon and focus on these 5 behavioral questions:

1.  **Where do you want it to live?** *(On my phone like a text contact, as a voice app, or on my computer for deep work?)*
2.  **What is the primary job?** *(Writing/drafting, researching, emotional support/venting, or managing tasks/calendar?)*
3.  **How do you want it to sound?** *(Professional and direct, warm and supportive, or witty and conversational?)*
4.  **Is memory important to you?** *(Do you want a fresh start every time, or someone who remembers your kids' names and your preferences?)*
5.  **Voice vs. Keyboard?** *(Do you want to talk to it out loud while driving, or will you mostly be typing?)*
