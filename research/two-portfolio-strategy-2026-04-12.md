## The Concept: "The Shield" vs "The Trough Hunter"
We have two paper portfolios, one 50-company watchlist, and a clear macro thesis (a potential 5-7% shock with a 2-4 week trough). We should run two opposing engines using the exact same watchlist to see which survives and thrives better under stress.

### Portfolio A: The Mechanical Shield (The $5k Framework)
- **Rules:** Execute the strict 0.5-2% risk swing trading model outlined by the Captain. 12-15 positions, fully deployed.
- **Hypothesis:** When the mayhem hits, the tight stops will act as a mechanical circuit breaker. It will take small, acceptable losses and automatically move to cash, preventing catastrophic drawdowns without requiring emotional decisions.

### Portfolio B: The Trough Hunter (The Macro Model)
- **Rules:** Trade the timeline. Hold a high percentage of cash right now. Wait for the 5-7% drop. 
- **Target:** Only deploy capital into the "Rails" (the heavily insulated companies like Thales, Honeywell, etc. from our watchlist) when we hit that 13-19 day trough.
- **Hypothesis:** Patience and cash preservation beat constant market exposure during geopolitical uncertainty.
