## PW — Feed the Soil (CLEAN FORMAT)

**Title:** Feed the Soil

**Description:** Everyone feeds their plants. Almost nobody feeds their soil — and that's why most gardens fail.

**Script:**
Everyone feeds their plants. Almost nobody feeds their soil.

We buy fertilizer, spray chemicals, wonder why nothing grows well. But the answer is under your feet. Healthy soil grows everything. Dead soil grows nothing.

Composting kitchen scraps. Banana peels. Egg shells. Coffee grounds. Let them break down and return to the earth. Cover bare soil with leaves — nature's blanket. It keeps moisture in, keeps weeds out, and feeds the worms who do the real work.

In three months, hard clay becomes soft, dark, living soil. You stop buying fertilizer. The plants feed themselves. The King called it working WITH nature, not against it.

Feed the soil. The soil feeds the plant. The plant feeds you.

## PW — Grow Up Not Out (CLEAN FORMAT)

**Title:** Grow Up, Not Out

**Description:** You don't need land to grow food — you need the right plants for the space you already have.

**Script:**
Plant it once, never buy it again.

Got a fence or wall? Long beans. Grow up, not out.

Vertical farming before it was trendy. Got new space. Papaya. One tree. Fruit in eight months. Shade for everything underneath.

King Rama the Ninth taught us — don't grow what's impressive. Grow what you eat. That's sufficiency.

## PW — Now What Do You Plant (CLEAN FORMAT)

**Title:** Now What Do You Plant

**Description:** You tested your soil. Now match the right plant to the space you actually have.

**Script:**
You tested your soil. Now what do you plant?

You don't need a farm. You just need the right plant for your space.

Got a balcony. Grow chilies. One plant, hundreds of chilies.

Got a windowsill. Spring onions. Cut them, they grow back. Free food that refuses to die.

Got one square meter. Morning glory. Plant it Monday, eat it Friday.

Got a small garden. Lemongrass. What do 10 bunches sell for? 50 Baht. Do that every day and you have 1500 Baht a month. From one crop.

Now multiply three crops. Four.

Your small garden is no longer a hobby. It's a micro business.

The sufficiency economy was never about staying small. It was about starting small and growing when you're ready. Not when someone tells you to. Not when the market demands it.

When your soil is ready, your first market day is your graduation. Everything before it was practice.

## PW — Your First Market Day (CLEAN FORMAT)

**Title:** Your First Market Day

**Description:** King Bhumibol called it practical worship — growing enough for your family, your neighbor, and then your freedom.

**Script:**
King Bhumibol called it pattibat bucha — practical worship.

Growing enough for your family, then growing enough for your neighbor. Then growing enough that the surplus becomes your freedom.

Start small. Know your costs.

A packet of seeds costs almost nothing. Water from the rain costs nothing. Your time — you were going to spend it anyway.

What did it cost to grow 10 bunches of morning glory? Almost zero. What do 10 bunches sell for? 50 Baht. Do that every day — 1500 Baht a month. From one crop.

Your first market day doesn't need to be perfect. A small table, a clean cloth. Whatever you grew, washed and arranged with care.

In Thailand they call this kai khong — selling things. But it's more than commerce. It's proof.

Proof that the soil answered. Proof that your hands can create something the world wants.

The first person who picks up your tomato, your herb, your chili, and reaches for their wallet.

That feeling no diploma gives you. No job title either.

Practical Worship. Honoring the land by learning from it.
