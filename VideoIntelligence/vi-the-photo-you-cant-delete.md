# VI — The Photo You Can't Delete

**Title:** The Photo You Can't Delete

**Description:** You delete a photo. It's gone, right? Not quite. Most people don't know where deleted photos actually go — and the ones who do know exactly where to look.

**Clean Script:**
You take a photo. You delete it. It's gone, right?

Not quite.

When you delete a photo from your phone, it goes to "Recently Deleted." Sits there for 30 days. Anyone who picks up your phone can see it.

Bank statements you photographed to send your accountant. Passport scans. Medical documents. That embarrassing screenshot you erased.

All sitting there. Waiting.

Most people don't know this folder exists. The ones who do — they know exactly where to look.

Here's the fix: Open your Photos app. Go to "Recently Deleted." Select all. Delete permanently.

Or just ask AI — "How do I permanently delete photos on my phone?"

It'll walk you through it. Step by step. No tech degree required.

Your privacy isn't complicated. It's just hidden.
