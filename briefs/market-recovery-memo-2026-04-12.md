# Market Recovery Dynamics - Historical Precedents
Date: 2026-04-12
Origin: Lung Nesto

## Core Data (Post-WWII Study - 20 Events)
- **Initial Drop:** Markets typically fall 5–7% from the start of the event to the trough.
- **Time to Bottom:** 13–19 trading days (approx. 2–4 weeks).
- **Time to Recovery (Pre-Event Levels):** Average 28 trading days (approx. 4–6 weeks). In 19 of 20 cases, recovery occurred within a month.
- **Broader Operations:** Average recovery of ~55 trading days (under 2 months).

## Key Dynamics
- **Short-Term Resilience:** The market reacts to the *initial shock and uncertainty*, not the full duration of the conflict.
- **Modifiers:** Oil price spikes or recessions can extend the recovery timeline.
- **Pricing:** Markets price outcomes rapidly. If action is decisive or contained, it often triggers relief rallies ("buy the rumor, sell the news" flips).
- **Conclusion:** Markets generally shake off military actions fast (1-2 months) provided broader economic fundamentals (earnings, policy) remain solid.
